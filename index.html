About me and my design identity

I want to be an industrial designer, I want to make products that are good for people and good for the world. I want to believe I can do this.

I have learned that designing a product is about more than what good it will do, but also what harm it can do, how it can hurt people and how it can hurt the environment. I have learned many questions you need to answer to make a product that causes as little harm as possible.

I have gained a better understanding of the environmental impact of design and design strategies. A paper shopping bag should be made of paper as its life cycle is short, it is worth compromising the integrity of the product to help the environment, the impact of a bag being slightly inconvenient the one time it is used is lesser than the impact of a bag that leaches chemicals and harms the environment effectively permanently. A back pack is different, it will be used for many years and to make it from paper would make it totally unusable, so then you would have to look at something else, a heavy cotton or even synthetics if you can justify it. This is also relevant for planned obsolescence, if you know that your product needs to fail than take that in to account, how will your product affect the environment when every one of them is thrown away and replaced what is the cost on the environment of making and destroying so many products, how can you minimise it?

Learning from the moxie robot by embedded it is incredibly important to not only consider the potential harm of your product but to actively work to stop it. You have to consider what will happen if you make a product that people will rely on. What will happen if the people can't access it anymore, not just if you go out of business like in the case of embedded but if the user can't afford it, if you just stop making/supporting it. What is going to happen to the people who need it when it is taken away? How can you prevent this pain and problem, can you even prevent this problem, and if you can't then should you be making it in the first place?
